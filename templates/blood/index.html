About Us

Who are Blood Works?

Blood Works is an organization dedicated to improving blood donation and related services. With a strong focus on promoting awareness and facilitating safe and efficient blood collection, Blood Works plays a vital role in ensuring a reliable supply of blood for transfusions and medical treatments. The organization collaborates with healthcare institutions, blood banks, and donors to establish best practices, educate the public, and streamline the donation process. Through innovative initiatives, such as community blood drives, donor recruitment campaigns, and advanced inventory management systems, Blood Works aims to enhance the availability and accessibility of life-saving blood products. Additionally, Blood Works actively engages in research and development to stay at the forefront of advancements in blood donation technology and techniques. By continuously striving for excellence in blood donation and related services, Blood Works contributes to saving lives and improving healthcare outcomes for individuals in need of blood transfusions.

The Donation Process

Requirements by blood type

These are some of the requirements donors must meet to be eligible to donate blood based on their donation type.

  • Donation frequency: Every 56 days*
  • You must be in good health and feeling well
  • You must be at least 16 years old in most states
  • You must weigh at least 110 lbs

If you have any questions click on the chatbot on the bottom right.

The Process

The blood donation process is divided into 4 steps:

Register yourself as a blood donor by providing the necessary personal information:

  • Name
  • Contact details
  • Blood type (if known)

Undergo a medical screening to assess your health and ensure blood safety:

  • Answer health-related questions truthfully
  • Get your blood pressure, pulse, and hemoglobin levels checked

Lie comfortably on a donation bed while a qualified phlebotomist collects your blood:

  • A small sample of blood will be taken for testing
  • About 350-450 ml of blood will be collected

Follow these post-donation care tips to ensure a smooth recovery:

  • Keep the donation site clean and dry
  • Drink plenty of fluids and avoid strenuous activities

Thank you for your valuable contribution and helping save lives!
